A digital art project transforming user-inputted text into Korean signboard images.

At the core of the project is the creation of a digital database by photographing and labeling individual letters on city signs. On the website developed based on this, users can enter their own sentences, which are automatically assembled and displayed as images of letters extracted from the signs.

Project <Ganpan> not only provides the ability to store images of sentences created by users in digital form, but also transforms these images into analog publications in the form of zines. This brings the artistic process from the digital back into physical form (in the hands of people) and explores how contemporary digital media can work complementarily with analog. Through the visual reconstruction of the text, the user is invited to take a fresh look at the spatial landscape of Korea, and at the same time, it presents the possibilities of digital art (website) and print media.


1. Reversing the methodology of AI
Figure 1. Diagram comparing AI Image Generation Model and Ganpan
The Ganpan project operates in the opposite direction of AI image generation. While AI learns from countless sources to create images with untraceable origins, Ganpan collects individual letters from Korean city signboards and clearly preserves their sources. User-inputted text is transformed into actual signboard letters, and clicking each letter reveals its original signboard context.
