A collaborative word-weaving game exploring poetic connections through custom similarity criteria

Word Wide Web is an interactive project developed during the "Human-Scale Natural Language Processing" course at SFPC. In this project, participants contributed unique text data, including my poetic essay inspired by Dai Yoko and George Eliot.

The text was broken down into individual words, categorized into 15 parts of speech, and further labeled based on six criteria: dog-cat, utilitarian-romantic, static-dynamic, concrete-intangible, satisfying-irritating, and hard-soft. Using these labels, I created a similarity measure that allows the system to suggest related words.

The game invites users to type in a word and quickly connect it to similar words, aiming to weave as many connections as possible within a time limit. As you play, explore whether the words you select feel related or different, and experience the unexpected connections in language.
