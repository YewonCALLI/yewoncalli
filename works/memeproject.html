An interactive website exploring meme origins, impact, and replication

The Meme Project is a fictional company focused on collecting and preserving memes. Drawing on the term "meme" as defined by Richard Dawkins in The Selfish Gene, this project views memes as cultural units that, like genes, replicate and spread independently. While genes are passed down biologically, memes circulate socially, often conveying humor or social critique with a playful edge.

The project‚Äôs website, accessible via QR code in the exhibition space, invites visitors to a virtual "museum" with three themed floors. The first floor is a kitchen-style space explaining meme origins; the second floor features the latest memes alongside a globe sculpture symbolizing meme spread; and the third floor allows visitors to create and save their own memes.

This project explores the self-replicating nature of memes and their potential reach, encouraging visitors to reflect on the humorous yet impactful role memes play in shaping cultural narratives.
